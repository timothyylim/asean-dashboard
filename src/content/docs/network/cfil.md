## Climate Finance Innovation Lab (CFIL) Malaysia

- flagship initiative of the **Joint Committee on Climate Change (JC3)**, co-chaired by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) and the Securities Commission Malaysia (SC)
- administered by **Bank Pembangunan Malaysia Berhad (BPMB)** — Malaysia's development bank
- strategic partner: **UNDP**
- website: https://www.bpmb.com.my/mycfil/ / https://www.mycfil.com
- contact: cfil@bpmb.com.my

## What they do

- collaborative platform to ideate, develop, and accelerate innovative climate finance solutions
- mobilises private and public finance to support Malaysia's transition to net zero
- adopts a whole-of-ecosystem approach — connecting climate projects with policy makers, experts, and funders
- Capital Solutioning Lab sessions to connect project owners with investors
- CFIL Accelerator Programme (with UNDP and CEDAR/SME Bank) — capacity building for selected projects
- blended finance structures, innovative mechanisms, and Islamic finance instruments

## Thematic areas

1. **Energy transition and green innovation**
2. **Sustainable agriculture**
3. **Circular economy and sustainable cities**
4. **Nature-based solutions and biodiversity**

## Flagship project: Coastal Oasis Initiative

- **Planters International Berhad** + Landasan Lumayan Sdn Bhd (LLSB) + Selangor Maritime Gateway (SMG)
- RM2.527 billion eco-restoration across 56 km Klang River corridor and 27,000 hectares of mangrove ecosystems
- integrates biodiversity restoration, waste recovery (biochar, plastic interception), and renewable energy
- uses AFAM platform (IoT, AI, remote sensing) for forest and mangrove monitoring
- supports Forest Conservation Certificate (FCC) framework

## Why it matters

- central node in Malaysia's climate finance ecosystem
- connected to government-backed development finance and international organisations
- bridges the gap between climate projects and capital
- good entry point for understanding who's doing what in Malaysia's climate space
